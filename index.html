⚠️
Lower Back Weakness — This is Your #1 Priority Fix
Weak lower back = injury risk on almost every gym exercise. Every session starts with dedicated back activation before anything else. We build it progressively: hyperextensions, cable rows, lat pulldowns, and deadlifts (light, technique only at first). A strong back also makes you look athletic without looking bulky.

Lower back protocol: These are therapeutic + strengthening. Use LIGHT weight with perfect form. Never ego-lift here. Lower back responds to volume and consistency, not heavy loads initially.

Squeeze glutes at top, hold 1 sec. Do NOT hyperextend past parallel. This is your lower back rehab + strengthen move.

Pull elbows back, squeeze shoulder blades. Sit tall — builds mid-back, removes "slouch". Light-moderate weight.

Pull to upper chest, lean back slightly. Builds the V-taper and upper back thickness. Do NOT pull behind neck.

Hinge at hips, bar slides down shins, feel hamstring stretch. Keep back flat throughout. This is the king of lower back strengthening — but learn it first.

Cable at eye level, pull to face with elbows high. Fixes posture, builds rear delts + rotator cuff.

Progress Timeline

Week 1–2
Adaptation. Soreness, especially legs and back. Normal. Drop 1–2kg (water weight). Learn RDL form with empty bar. Hyperextensions daily.
Week 3–4
Lower back noticeably stronger. Real fat loss starts: 0.5–1kg/week. You'll notice less back fatigue. Pullups improving on assisted machine.
Month 2
~4kg down. Clothes fit differently. Back no longer a weakness — it's becoming a strength. Start adding weight to RDL and cable rows.
Month 3
~7kg down. 84kg range. Visible body change. Posture visibly improved from all the back work. Start attempting full unassisted pullups.
Month 4–5
~12–14kg down. ~77–79kg range. Athletic, lean look emerging. Back is one of your strong points now. Begin introducing calisthenics fundamentals — you're ready.
Month 6+
75kg target range. Transition to calisthenics-focused training. Your gym base makes you far better prepared for pullups, dips, L-sits, and eventually gymnastic movements than you'd be without it.
